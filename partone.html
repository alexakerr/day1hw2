Downward Dog

also known as Adho Mukha Svanasana in Sanskrit, is a foundational yoga pose that is commonly practiced in various yoga styles

Downward Dog Instructions

  • Starting Position: Begin on your hands and knees, with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips
  • Hand Placement: Spread your fingers wide apart with your index fingers parallel or slightly turned out, and press firmly into the ground with your palms
  • Foot Placement: Curl your toes under, then lift your hips up and back toward the ceiling, straightening your legs as much as you can without locking your knees
  • Hip Alignment: Your body should form an inverted V shape. Keep your heels reaching toward the ground, but it's okay if they don't touch the floor, especially if you're a beginner. Allow your head to hang freely between your arms, with your gaze directed toward your feet or navel
